The world is driven by massive, complex systems, and I view myself as just one small cog trying to help the wheels of healthcare and sustainable tech turn a little smoother. My day-to-day focuses on researching and building physical microfluidic hardware using a variety of fabrication techniques to bridge the gap between early concepts and functional devices. This blog is simply my corner to document that process, exploring the quiet, often messy mechanics of how ideas are actually translated into the real world.
