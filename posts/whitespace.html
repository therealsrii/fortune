In modern web design, clutter is the default. We are bombarded with notifications, popups, and sidebars competing for our attention. Minimalist design is a rebellion against this noise. And its primary weapon is whitespace.

Why Space Matters

Whitespace, or negative space, is the area between design elements. It is not empty space; it is a powerful layout tool that provides breathing room. It creates visual hierarchy, guides the user's eye, and dramatically improves readability.

"Whitespace is like the silence in music; it is what makes the notes stand out."

When we allow space around text and images, we signal their importance. A single image centered on a white page feels premium and intentional. A crowded grid of images feels cheap and chaotic. For "Cog in the Wheel," we embrace the silence of space.
