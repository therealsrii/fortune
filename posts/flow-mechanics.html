At the microscale, fluid physics behaves counterintuitively. Gravity becomes negligible, while surface tension and viscosity dominate. This shifts fluid flow into a strictly laminar regime, where turbulence does not exist.

Designing with Laminar Flow

Laminar flow allows us to place two different fluid streams side by side in the same channel without mixing, except through molecular diffusion. This enables precise control over chemical gradients, allowing us to expose biological cells to stable micro-environments.

Understanding and harnessing these micro-scale flow mechanics is critical to developing sustainable biotechnology platforms that require minimal reagent volumes and power.
