Microfluidics is shifting from bespoke, one-off chips to modular hardware architectures. By developing standardized connection blocks and interchangeable modules, we can build complex assay systems with the ease of assembly blocks.

The Lego Brick of Biology

Our research focuses on creating reliable micro-connectors that can route fluids between different functional blocks—pumps, mixers, and detectors—without leakage. This modularity allows us to iterate on design concepts rapidly, replacing single blocks without redesigning the entire system.

As we bridge the gap between proof-of-concept and medical application, modular hardware will lower the barrier to entry for clinical laboratories around the world, enabling accessible point-of-care diagnostics.
