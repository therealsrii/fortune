Street photography is a study in transient geometry. It is about capturing the fleeting alignment of light, shadow, and human form within the rigid lines of the built environment.

The High-Contrast World

By shooting in high-contrast black-and-white, we strip away the distraction of color, revealing the underlying architecture of a scene. A shaft of morning light cutting between two buildings becomes a physical wall. A shadow cast by a passerby becomes a solid form.

"Light makes photography, but shadows define it."

This series of street forms captures the quiet, overlooked corners of our cities—places where light and shadow play a perpetual game of geometry. It is an exploration of finding order in urban chaos.
