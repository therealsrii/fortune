Isolating individual cells from a heterogeneous mixture is a fundamental requirement for single-cell genomics and cancer research. Microfluidic sorting systems offer high-throughput, low-damage selection based on physical or optical properties.

Optical Sorting Geometry

Using laser-induced sorting or automated acoustic waves, we can direct target cells into collector channels with millisecond response times. The geometry of the sorting junction is critical to preventing cell adhesion and ensuring high purity.

By studying cell sorting patterns under high-speed microscope imaging, we refine the channel geometry to optimize fluid flow and cell survival rates, translating engineering concepts into tools for life-saving diagnostics.
